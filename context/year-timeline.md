## Trimester / assessment bands

- **Trimester 1** (Wks 1-11): Units 1-2; ends ~Nov 14.
- **Trimester 2** (Wks 12-23): Units 3-4; Interim 2 in Wk 18; ends Mar 5-6.
- **Trimester 3** (Wks 24-34): Units 5-6; PSAT 8/9 in April, state testing Apr-May. New content ends Wk 31 (May 22); Wk 32 = DC trip, Wk 33 = catch-up/flex, Wk 34 = review & finals. Ends June 12.

## End-of-year guardrails (so Unit 6 finishes)

Unit 6 ran out of time last year. The back end is now hard-fenced; treat these as fixed, not aspirational:

1. **New content stops May 22 (Wk 31).** Both tracks must have Unit 6 fully taught by the Friday before the trip. Schedule U6 to land here, not at the nominal Wk 32-33.
2. **DC trip week (May 25-29, Wk 32) is non-instructional.** Memorial Day takes Monday; travel and chaperoning take the rest. Plan zero new content and no assessments.
3. **Catch-up / flex week (June 1-5, Wk 33) is reserve, not content.** It is the single designated buffer for slippage from state testing and earlier units. Leave it unplanned on purpose: if U6 is on track, use it for reteach / SBG retakes; if not, it finishes U6.
4. **Finals week (June 8-12, Wk 34): review and final assessments only.**

Consequence: ~31 usable content weeks, not 34. To keep U6 whole, the lost time must come from an earlier unit, not from U6. Gen Ed U6 (Midsummer) is the tightest squeeze; if U5 Animal Farm runs to its old Wk 29 end, U6 collapses to two weeks. Either end U5 by Wk 28 or trim a longer earlier unit (Chew On This at 8 weeks, or Chains) to buy U6 a real three-week run.

## Seams to watch

1. The source gives Gen Ed U3 Chains as both "Weeks 12-17" (teaching-context.md) and "Weeks 12-18, Interim 2 in Week 18" (planning-guide.md). This timeline uses the planning-guide version (Wks 12-18), the one with calendar integration.
2. Shared texts fall in different weeks across tracks: Short Stories aligns at Wks 1-3, but Animal Farm is Gen Ed Wks 24-29 vs. Accel Wks 28-33. The shared-text efficiency really only lands at the start of the year, not the end.
