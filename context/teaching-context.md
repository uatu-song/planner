# Plymouth Scholars Charter Academy - 8th Grade ELA Teaching Context

## Quick Reference Summary
**Teacher**: 8th Grade ELA teacher implementing Standards-Based Grading (SBG) in a high-performing charter school with significant implementation challenges.

**School**: Plymouth Scholars Charter Academy, Plymouth, MI
- Ranked #1 Charter Elementary & Middle School in Michigan (Niche)
- 74% math proficiency, 76% reading proficiency (vs. state averages of 33% and 42%)
- Managed by National Heritage Academies (NHA)
- Demographics: 51% Asian, 27% White, 9% African American, 9% Two or more races, 4% Hispanic

## Key Challenges & Context

### 1. **Standards-Based Grading Implementation Issues**
- **Philosophy vs. Practice Gap**: School claims SBG but implementation is inconsistent
- **Teacher Resistance**: Many teachers prefer traditional average-based grading
- **Parent/Family Pressure**: Families want "more grade opportunities" (misunderstanding SBG)
- **System Limitations**: NHA's proprietary gradebook lacks essential SBG features:
  - Can only sort by name and high/low grades
  - No filtering by standards, assignment types, or date ranges
  - Makes tracking progress by standard nearly impossible

### 2. **Workload Concerns**
- Excessive duties beyond teaching
- Administrative tasks, PD, and culture-building events overwhelming even veteran staff
- Despite curriculum support, total responsibilities have reached breaking point

### 3. **Positive Aspects**
- Comprehensive curriculum provided with slides and lesson plans
- All materials aligned to CCR standards
- Pre-made assessments reduce planning time
- Rich content for critical thinking and writing skills
- Clear year-long scope and sequence

## Curriculum Overview

### Year-Long Structure
- **Essential Question**: "How much control do I have over my own life?"
- **34 weeks** divided into 6 major units
- **Week 0**: Relationship building and classroom culture establishment

### Unit Progression
1. **Short Stories** (Weeks 1-3): Foundation skills - plot, theme, characterization, POV
2. **Chew On This** (Weeks 4-11): Argument analysis, evaluating evidence
3. **Chains** (Weeks 12-17): Historical fiction, character perspectives, mood/tone
4. **Frederick Douglass** (Weeks 19-23): Informational text analysis, central idea development
5. **Animal Farm** (Weeks 24-29): Allegory, satire, propaganda, theme development
6. **A Midsummer Night's Dream** (Weeks 30-32): Shakespeare, allusions, text structure

### Major Themes Across Units
- Personal responsibility and control
- Power, manipulation, and propaganda
- Education as freedom
- Actions and consequences
- Social inequality and justice
- Coming of age and identity

## Week 0 Purpose & Connection
Week 0 serves as foundation by:
- Building relationships for year-long literature discussions
- Establishing classroom norms and procedures
- Introducing SBG concepts through games
- Creating safe environment for risk-taking
- Previewing major themes through activities
- Addressing workload concerns through efficient systems

## Teaching Philosophy & Approach
- **SBG Advocate**: Believes in showing mastery/highest achievement vs. averaging
- **Relationship-Focused**: SBG approach builds better relationships with struggling students
- **Student-Centered**: Emphasizes student voice in creating norms and procedures
- **Growth Mindset**: Focus on learning from mistakes and continuous improvement

## Key Pedagogical Elements
- **Habits of Discussion**: Emphasized throughout curriculum
- **Evidence-Based Analysis**: Students must cite textual evidence
- **Standards Alignment**: All units clearly mapped to specific standards
- **EXCEL-ERATE Program**: Extensions for advanced learners
- **Writing Integration**: Each literature unit paired with writing instruction

## Implementation Strategies
- Use Week 0 to build buy-in for SBG before academic pressure
- Create classroom systems that work around gradebook limitations
- Focus on relationship building to ease classroom management
- Leverage provided curriculum while maintaining teaching autonomy
- Balance school expectations with pedagogical best practices
